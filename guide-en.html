User Guide — how to connect Zoho, enter your forecast, and read every view of the platform.
AspenView Technology Partners · Sales Forecasting Platform

1What this platform does

A live, browser-based forecasting platform that replaces the manual Excel workbook. It connects to Zoho CRM, lets you layer your forecasting inputs on top of the CRM data, and keeps the whole team looking at the same numbers in real time.

Before — ExcelNow — Platform
Deals copied by hand from ZohoDeals pulled automatically from Zoho
Each person had their own fileEveryone shares one live, auto-synced view
Margin not trackedMargin built in — feeds cash-flow
Static monthly snapshotRolling 3-month forecast that moves with one click
No reliability measureForecast-accuracy bands by horizon

Two kinds of data live together

  • From Zoho (automatic): deal name, account/client, stage, owner (BDE), MRR, probability, close date, account status, and the Target Margin Band — refreshed every time you Sync. Zoho is the source of truth.
  • Manual (you enter): monthly headcount per deal, MRR Aureum (fallback when Zoho's MRR is blank), win-rate adjustments, margin overrides, the Forecast and Super-Deal flags, and the Super-Deal qualitative assessment.
The key ideaThe manual inputs turn raw CRM data into a real, decision-ready forecast. The Playground tab lets you draft changes side-by-side with Zoho and push approved updates back to the CRM in one click.

2Getting in

  1. Open the platform URL in Chrome or Edge.
  2. Click Sign in with Google.
  3. Choose your @aspenview.com account. Only @aspenview.com accounts are allowed.
  4. You land on the Overview tab.

The header (top-right)

IndicatorMeaning

Name · Log OutYour identity and sign-out.
Shared in real timeEverything you edit is shared. If the manager changes a number, you see it within seconds — and vice-versa.

3Connecting Zoho

The Zoho token lasts about one hour, so you'll reconnect occasionally.

  1. In the header, click the Zoho · Disconnected pill.
  2. On Zoho, click Accept to authorize access to Deals (read + write) and Accounts (read).
  3. You return; the pill turns green: Zoho · Connected.
Write scopeThe platform requests ZohoCRM.modules.Deals.WRITE so the Playground's Push approved to Zoho button can update records. If your Zoho admin hasn't enabled write yet, the rest of the platform still works; only the push action will surface a clear message asking the admin to enable the scope.

Pulling the deals

  1. Go to Aureum Demand Forecast.
  2. Click ↻ Sync from Zoho (top-right).
  3. A summary shows how many deals were pulled, matched, skipped, and how many manual deals are preserved.
  4. Click Accept. The list refreshes with the latest from Zoho.

Which deals come in — only these stages (everything except Closed Lost):

SQL - Solution Discovery · Stage 2 (WTW) · Commercial Design · Recruitment (Path 2.1) · P.S (Path 2.2) · Pricing · Paper Process / Contracting · Closed Won

Your work is safe on re-syncMonthly headcount, MRR Aureum, win-rate edits, and the Forecast/Super flags stay attached to each deal. Only the Zoho fields (stage, amount, owner) get refreshed.

4The 5-phase forecasting model

Every Zoho deal is bucketed into exactly one of five phases. Phases drive what enters the forecast and what is captured separately in cash-flow.

PhaseZoho stages that map hereForecasted by default?
Phase 1 — SQLSQL - Solution Discovery · Stage 2 (WTW)No — early pipeline, too uncertain to forecast
Phase 2 — RecruitmentRecruitment (Path 2.1) · P.S (Path 2.2) · Commercial DesignYes
Phase 3 — PricingPricingYes
Phase 4 — Legal ContractingPaper Process / ContractingYes
Phase 5 — Closed WonClosed WonNo — already won, captured in cash flow directly

The phase filter strip at the top of Aureum Demand Forecast lets you toggle which phases enter the forecast on the fly. Defaults are tuned to Michael's preferences: Recruitment + Pricing + Legal Contracting ON, SQL and Closed Won OFF.

5Build & Invoice — not Close

⚡ Headcount is when people START BILLING — not when the deal closes.A 250-person engagement may close in June but ramp 35 → 70 → 70 over the following weeks. Enter those headcounts in the month they bill, not the month the contract closes. This framing applies to every tab where you type headcount by month.

6The tabs, one by one

6.1 — Overview

Your executive snapshot. KPI cards (deals, 3-month headcount, adjusted revenue, margin $), the Anchor month picker, a 🔒 Lock {month} forecast button, the rolling 3-month window table, and the Forecast Reliability bands (M+0 / M+1 / M+2).

6.2 — Pipeline

The C-suite snapshot — the same kind of view investors see. Four KPI cards (Total Deals, Total Headcount, Total Pipeline MRR, Total Pipeline ARR) and a phase-by-phase breakdown table with deal count, HC, MRR, ARR, and % of total MRR. Click any phase row to focus the deal breakdown below on that single phase. The bottom cards segment deals by age: Healthy (<3 mo) / Active Pipeline (3-6 mo) / Slow-moving (6-12 mo) / Critical (>1 yr).

6.3 — Aureum Demand Forecast (the master list)

Where everything starts. Every Zoho deal in the allowed stages is one row, grouped by account. The header row is sticky as you scroll. The phase filter strip at the top lets you scope the table to specific phases.

+ New Deal opens Zoho's new-deal form in a new tab. New deals are always created in Zoho — never locally.

6.4 — 3 mo. revenue (rolling 1-3 month forecast with Lock)

Deep-dive on the next 3 months. Only deals flagged Fcst appear here. For each, across the 3-month window: HC, Proj. Rev (HC × MRR/head), Adj. Rev (× Win %), Margin $ (× Margin %).

🔒 Lock {month} forecast — once the numbers are right, click to snapshot them as an immutable commitment. The locked snapshot is what the accuracy bands compare against when actuals roll in.

Apply BDE accuracy haircut — checkbox that multiplies each cell by that BDE's historical accuracy at that horizon. Falls back to the aggregate band when a BDE has no history yet.

6.5 — 12-Month Plan (months 4-12)

The mid-term view. Per-deal headcount ramp by month for months 4 through 12 (so 9 months ahead). Revenue is computed from HC × MRR/head automatically. The Build & Invoice reminder is reinforced here because it's the most common point of confusion.

6.6 — Long-term Growth (months 13-24)

No per-deal forecasting at this horizon. Pick a baseline (the last mid-term month, or the 9-month average) and a monthly growth rate (0.5%, 1%, 2%, 3%, 5%, 7%, 10%). The model projects 12 months of revenue forward. Used by Finance and the CEO for long-term planning where per-deal certainty doesn't exist.

6.7 — Playground (Zoho vs Manual, with approve & push)

The scenario sandbox. Side-by-side, per deal: what Zoho says today vs what you've drafted in Aureum for Headcount, MRR, Win % and Margin %. Each diff is highlighted with a Zoho → Playground arrow.

  1. Draft changes in Aureum — they show up here automatically.
  2. Approve the deals whose drafts are ready (per-row checkbox). Approval is shared with the team.
  3. Click ↗ Push approved to Zoho. The platform sends a PATCH to Zoho per approved deal — updating Headcounts, MRR, Probability, and Target_Margin_Band.
Zoho is the system of recordIf your OAuth client doesn't have Deals.WRITE yet, the push action explains what your Zoho admin needs to enable.

6.8 — Forecast Accuracy (with embedded Trend section)

Reliability bands by horizon. Lock a forecast → wait for the month to close → Enter Actuals → bands populate as mean ± standard deviation per horizon. Defaults until history builds: M+0 = 80% ±5, M+1 = 75% ±10, M+2 = 30% ±20.

Accuracy by BDE table breaks the bands down per person at each horizon — Michael's ask: "Don is 90% accurate one month out, 80% at two months, 50% at three."

📥 Backfill historical data — opens a form where you enter past months by hand to seed the accuracy bands without waiting 3+ months. One row per (month, BDE), with what was forecast at start of that month / 1 month earlier / 2 months earlier, plus the actual.

At the bottom: an embedded Forecast Trend section with toggle Weekly / Monthly / Quarterly and three line charts showing Deals forecasted, Total Headcount, and Adj. Revenue over time. Solid line = forecast; dashed green line = actual (filled from Actuals + backfill). Where the lines diverge tells you where the forecast was optimistic or sandbagged.

6.9 — Super-Deals

Qualitative assessment of programs with 100+ HC. Each program is a row with editable, color-coded dropdowns: Deal Confidence, Start Readiness, Readiness Gap, Speed Sensitivity, Ramp Duration, First Phase Timing, Phase Risk Concentration, Program Horizon, Deployment Map. Edit Peak HC / Phase 1 / Activation / Notes with the ✎ icon.

6.10 — Active Customers — Actuals

Customers currently producing revenue (Active / Expanding / Aureum) plus Closed Won. Current HC, MRR/head, monthly $. HC falls back to the Zoho Headcounts field when no monthly HC is planned — marked with a small ·z beside the number.

6.11 — Cleanup

Reconcile deals from the original Excel that didn't auto-match to a Zoho record. For each unmatched deal, the platform shows the best Zoho candidate from the same account (active deals first, then Closed Lost in red) with a Match %.

  • Merge — point the row at the chosen Zoho deal; the row becomes synced going forward.
  • Kill — delete the ghost row if it doesn't correspond to anything in Zoho.

The goal: empty this tab so every deal is linked to Zoho.

6.12 — Settings (Stage Rules + Integrations)

Stage Rules live here now (formerly a standalone tab). The defaults that fill in Win % and Margin % when a deal doesn't have its own. Cascade order: per-deal override → Zoho (Probability + Target_Margin_Band) → Super-Deal rule → Stage rule → default.

Plus: Zoho integration status + field mapping (MRR, Headcounts, Target_Margin_Band, Account_Status), Export / Import JSON for backup, and the ↻ Replay tour button.

★ Admin Panel (header button, admins only)

The gold "★ Admin" button next to Guide in the top-right opens the Admin Panel — visible only to managers and emails that have been granted admin via the panel. Inside:

  • Team — every user who has signed in, with last-active, status, and per-row Grant / Revoke admin buttons. Add admins by typing their email and clicking + Add admin.
  • Activity — live stream of all platform events: Zoho syncs, Pushes to Zoho, snapshots, sign-ins, deal edits, admin changes.
  • Errors — auto-captured Zoho REST failures with code, endpoint, message, and actor.

7How the forecast actually moves

ZOHO ──(Sync)──► Aureum Demand Forecast ◄──(Push approved)──┐ │ │ you enter: Monthly HC + MRR Aureum (if Zoho blank) + Win % + Margin % you check: Forecast ☑ Super ☑ │ │ │ ▼ │ Forecast ☑ deals ──► 3 mo. revenue (HC × MRR/head × Win% × Margin%) ──► 🔒 Lock │ ▼ Forecast Accuracy bands (per BDE) deals 4-12 mo ──► 12-Month Plan ▲ growth % ──► Long-term Growth (13-24 mo) │ Super ☑ deals ──► Super-Deals rollup │ Enter Actuals diffs vs Zoho ──► Playground ──(approve)─────────────────┘ │ ▼ Overview + Pipeline (5-phase investor view)

The levers you pull: Monthly HC drives projected revenue · MRR (Zoho wins if set, else Aureum) is the price per deal · Win % discounts by probability · Margin % produces the cash-flow number · Anchor month slides the window · the Phase filter + Forecast/Super checkboxes decide what shows up where · 🔒 Lock commits a forecast as the immutable accuracy baseline.

9Guided tour for new users

The first time anyone signs in, a 12-step walk-through pops up explaining each module in plain language. Buttons: Skip tour, ← Previous, Next →. A progress bar at the top of the card shows how far along you are.

To replay the tour at any time — useful for new hires or as a refresher — go to Settings and click ↻ Replay tour. The tour switches the underlying tab as you progress so you see each module on the actual screen behind the explanation card.

11Backup

Firestore keeps the shared copy, but export a backup periodically: Settings → ⬇ Export all data (JSON). To restore: Settings → ⬆ Import data (JSON).

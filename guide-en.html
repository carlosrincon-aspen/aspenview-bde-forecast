User Guide — how to connect Zoho, enter your forecast, and read every view of the platform.
AspenView Technology Partners · Sales Forecasting Platform

1What this platform does

A live, browser-based forecasting platform that replaces the manual Excel workbook. It connects to Zoho CRM, lets you layer your forecasting inputs on top of the CRM data, and keeps the whole team looking at the same numbers in real time.

Before — ExcelNow — Platform
Deals copied by hand from ZohoDeals pulled automatically from Zoho
Each person had their own fileEveryone shares one live, auto-synced view
Margin not trackedMargin built in — feeds cash-flow
Static monthly snapshotRolling 3-month forecast that moves with one click
No reliability measureForecast-accuracy bands by horizon

Two kinds of data live together

  • From Zoho (automatic): deal name, account/client, stage, owner (BDE), MRR, probability, close date, account status, and the Target Margin Band — refreshed every time you Sync. Zoho is the source of truth.
  • Manual (you enter): monthly headcount per deal, MRR Aureum (fallback when Zoho's MRR is blank), win-rate adjustments, margin overrides, the Forecast and Super-Deal flags, and the Super-Deal qualitative assessment.
The key ideaThe manual inputs turn raw CRM data into a real, decision-ready forecast. The Playground tab lets you draft changes side-by-side with Zoho and push approved updates back to the CRM in one click.

2Getting in

  1. Open the platform URL in Chrome or Edge.
  2. Click Sign in with Google.
  3. Choose your @aspenview.com account. Only @aspenview.com accounts are allowed.
  4. You land on the Overview tab.

The header (top-right)

IndicatorMeaning
Sync · LiveYour data is connected and shared. Saving… = pushing a change; Live (updated by …) = a teammate's change just arrived.
ZohoConnected / Disconnected — click to connect or disconnect Zoho.
Name · Log OutYour identity and sign-out.
Shared in real timeEverything you edit is shared. If the manager changes a number, you see it within seconds — and vice-versa.

3Connecting Zoho

The Zoho token lasts about one hour, so you'll reconnect occasionally.

  1. In the header, click the Zoho · Disconnected pill.
  2. On Zoho, click Accept to authorize access to Deals (read + write) and Accounts (read).
  3. You return; the pill turns green: Zoho · Connected.
Write scopeThe platform requests ZohoCRM.modules.Deals.WRITE so the Playground's Push approved to Zoho button can update records. If your Zoho admin hasn't enabled write yet, the rest of the platform still works; only the push action will surface a clear message asking the admin to enable the scope.

Pulling the deals

  1. Go to Aureum Demand Forecast.
  2. Click ↻ Sync from Zoho (top-right).
  3. A summary shows how many deals were pulled, matched, skipped, and how many manual deals are preserved.
  4. Click Accept. The list refreshes with the latest from Zoho.

Which deals come in — only these stages (everything except Closed Lost):

SQL - Solution Discovery · Stage 2 (WTW) · Commercial Design · Recruitment (Path 2.1) · P.S (Path 2.2) · Pricing · Paper Process / Contracting · Closed Won

Your work is safe on re-syncMonthly headcount, MRR Aureum, win-rate edits, and the Forecast/Super flags stay attached to each deal. Only the Zoho fields (stage, amount, owner) get refreshed.

4The tabs, one by one

4.1 — Overview

Your executive snapshot: KPI cards (deals, 3-month headcount, adjusted revenue, margin $), the Anchor month picker, the rolling 3-month window table, and the Forecast Reliability bands (M0 / M+1 / M+2).

4.2 — Aureum Demand Forecast (the master list)

Where everything starts. Every Zoho deal in the allowed stages is one row, grouped by account so all deals of a client sit together. The header row is sticky: it stays visible as you scroll, so you never lose which month a column is.

Search & filters: the search box filters by deal, client, or BDE. The BDE / Stage / Status / In Excel dropdowns are multi-select — pick two BDEs at once, etc. A badge shows the count; "✕ Clear" resets a filter.

+ New Deal opens Zoho's new-deal form in a new tab. New deals are always created in Zoho — never locally — so the CRM stays the system of record.

4.3 — 3 mo. revenue (the rolling forecast)

Shows only the deals you checked "Fcst". For each, across the 3-month window: HC, Proj. Rev (HC × MRR/head), Adj. Rev (× Win %), Margin $ (× Margin %). Win % and Margin % sit on the LEFT for fast scanning. Totals roll up at the bottom and in three summary cards.

This is the number the company seesIt only contains deals you deliberately flagged — nothing leaks in by accident.

4.4 — Playground (Zoho vs Manual, with approve & push)

The scenario sandbox. Side-by-side, per deal, you see what Zoho says today and what you've drafted manually for Headcount, MRR, Win % and Margin %. Every difference is highlighted with a Zoho → Playground arrow.

  1. Draft changes on the Aureum tab as usual — they show up here as diffs.
  2. Approve the deals whose drafts are ready (per-row checkbox). Approval is shared with the team.
  3. Click ↗ Push approved to Zoho. The platform sends a PATCH to Zoho for each approved deal — updating Total_Headcount, MRR, Probability, and Target_Margin_Band — then clears the approved flag and stamps pushedAt.
Zoho is the system of recordPushed values become the new Zoho truth on the next Sync. If your OAuth client doesn't have the Deals.WRITE scope yet, the push action explains exactly what your Zoho admin needs to enable.

4.5 — Forecast Accuracy

Measures how reliable the forecast has been — not a way to grade anyone. The platform snapshots the forecast monthly; when a month closes you Enter Actuals, and the bands recompute as mean ± standard deviation per horizon. Defaults until history builds: M0 = 80% ±5, M+1 = 75% ±10, M+2 = 30% ±20.

4.6 — Super-Deals

The Aureum-tier program assessment. Each program is a row with editable, color-coded dropdowns: Deal Confidence, Start Readiness, Readiness Gap, Speed Sensitivity, Ramp Duration, First Phase Timing, Phase Risk Concentration, Program Horizon, Deployment Map. Edit Peak HC / Phase 1 / Activation / Notes with ✎. A deal-level rollup below sums every deal flagged "Super".

4.7 — Fulfillment View

Headcount by deal-type (Active Roles + Super-Deal Roles) for delivery/recruiting prioritization. Filter by BDE and search.

4.8 — MRR Forecast 2026

Adjusted revenue by client across all 12 months. Read it by quarter by grouping the relevant month columns (Jan–Mar = Q1, etc.). Search by client.

4.9 — Active Customers — Actuals

Customers currently producing revenue (Active / Expanding / Aureum) plus Closed Won: current headcount, MRR per head, monthly $. Filter by BDE and status.

4.10 — Stage Rules

The defaults that fill in Win % and Margin % when a deal doesn't have its own. Edit the table per stage; both columns are editable. The cascade is:

  1. Per-deal override (Win % / Margin % typed on the deal) →
  2. Zoho (Probability for Win, Target_Margin_Band for Margin) →
  3. Super-Deal rule (if the deal is flagged Super) →
  4. Stage rule (matches the deal's current Zoho stage) →
  5. Default fallback.

4.11 — Cleanup

Reconcile deals that came from the original Excel but couldn't be matched to a Zoho record. For each unmatched deal, the platform shows the best Zoho candidate from the same account (active deals first, then Closed Lost in red) with a Match %.

  • Merge — point the row at the chosen Zoho deal; the row becomes synced going forward.
  • Kill — delete the ghost row if it doesn't correspond to anything in Zoho.

The goal is to eventually empty this tab — once every deal is linked to Zoho, Don's Excel can be retired.

4.12 — Settings

Zoho integration & field mapping (MRR, Total_Headcount, Target_Margin_Band, Account_Status), data Export / Import (JSON) for backup, reset, and the daily-workflow reminder.

5How the forecast actually moves

ZOHO ──(Sync)──► Aureum Demand Forecast ◄──(Push approved)──┐ │ │ you enter: Monthly HC + MRR Aureum (if Zoho blank) + Win % + Margin % you check: Forecast ☑ Super ☑ │ │ │ ▼ │ Forecast ☑ deals ──► 3 mo. revenue (HC × MRR/head × Win% × Margin%) Super ☑ deals ──► Super-Deals rollup │ diffs vs Zoho ──► Playground ──(approve)─────────────┘ │ ▼ Overview KPIs + Forecast Accuracy bands + MRR Forecast

The levers you pull: Monthly HC drives projected revenue · MRR (Zoho wins if set, else Aureum) is the price per deal · Win % discounts by probability · Margin % produces the cash-flow number · Anchor month slides the window · the Forecast/Super checkboxes decide what shows up where.

7Tips & troubleshooting

SymptomFix
Revenue shows $0No MRR Zoho and no MRR Aureum — enter an MRR Aureum value or set the Amount in Zoho.
A deal isn't in 3 mo. revenueCheck its Fcst box and confirm it has headcount in the anchor window.
Sign-in "not authorized"You used a non-@aspenview.com account. Switch accounts.
Zoho sync fails (401)The 1-hour token expired. Click the Zoho pill → reconnect.
Push to Zoho says "scope missing"The OAuth client lacks ZohoCRM.modules.Deals.WRITE. Ask the Zoho admin to enable it at api-console.zoho.com, then Disconnect + Connect from the Zoho pill so the new permission takes effect.
A deal shows ⚠ missing marginIt's in Commercial Design or later but has no margin. Set the Target_Margin_Band in Zoho, or type a value in the Margin % cell.
A deal shows ⚠ none for BDENo deal in that account has an owner in Zoho. Assign one in Zoho, then re-sync.
Changes not showing elsewhereConfirm both screens show Sync · Live; hard-refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R).

8Backup

Firestore keeps the shared copy, but export a backup periodically: Settings → ⬇ Export all data (JSON). To restore: Settings → ⬆ Import data (JSON).

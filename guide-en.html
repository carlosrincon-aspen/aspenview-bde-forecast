1What this platform does

A live, browser-based forecasting platform that replaces the manual Excel workbook. It connects to Zoho CRM, lets you layer your forecasting inputs on top of the CRM data, and keeps the whole team looking at the same numbers in real time.

Before — ExcelNow — Platform
Deals copied by hand from ZohoDeals pulled automatically from Zoho
Each person had their own fileEveryone shares one live, auto-synced view
Margin not trackedMargin built in — feeds cash-flow
Static monthly snapshotRolling 3-month forecast that moves with one click
No reliability measureForecast-accuracy bands by horizon

Two kinds of data live together

  • From Zoho (automatic): deal name, account/client, stage, owner (BDE), MRR · ARR · TCV, contract length, expected start date, probability (win %), close date, and account status — refreshed every time you Sync. Zoho is the source of truth.
  • Manual (you enter): monthly headcount per deal, $/head (when you want to override), the actual margin once a deal is priced, win-rate adjustments, and the Forecast flag. (The Super-Deal flag is automatic — any deal with 100+ headcount. Margin is calculated, not in Zoho.)
The key ideaThe manual inputs turn raw CRM data into a real, decision-ready forecast. The Playground tab lets you draft changes side-by-side with Zoho and push approved updates back to the CRM in one click.

2Getting in

  2. Click Sign in with Google.
  3. Choose your @aspenview.com account. Access is invite-only: an admin must have added you first (as an admin or an observer). A plain @aspenview.com account that wasn't invited is turned away with a message to ask an admin.
  4. You land on the Overview tab.

3Connecting Zoho

The Zoho token lasts about one hour, so you'll reconnect occasionally.

  1. In the header, click the Zoho · Disconnected pill.
  2. On Zoho, click Accept to authorize access to Deals (read + write) and Accounts (read).
  3. You return; the pill turns green: Zoho · Connected.
Write scope — enabledThe platform requests ZohoCRM.modules.Deals.WRITE so the Playground's Push approved to Zoho button can update records. Write access is enabled. Because Zoho's token is per user, each person who will push authorizes once: click the Zoho pill → Disconnect → Connect → Accept (grant Deals read + write). Observers don't push, so they don't need this.

Pulling the deals

  1. Go to Aureum Demand Forecast.
  2. Click ↻ Sync from Zoho (top-right).
  3. A summary shows how many deals were pulled, matched, skipped, and how many manual deals are preserved.
  4. Click Accept. The list refreshes with the latest from Zoho.

Which deals come in — only these stages (everything except Closed Lost):

SQL - Solution Discovery · Stage 2 (WTW) · Commercial Design · Recruitment (Path 2.1) · P.S (Path 2.2) · Pricing · Paper Process / Contracting · Closed Won

Your work is safe on re-syncMonthly headcount, $/head, actual margin, win-rate edits, and the Forecast flag stay attached to each deal. Only the Zoho fields (stage, MRR/ARR/TCV, owner, dates) get refreshed.

4The 5-phase forecasting model

Every Zoho deal is bucketed into exactly one of five phases. Phases drive what enters the forecast and what is captured separately in cash-flow.

PhaseZoho stages that map hereForecasted by default?
Phase 1 — SQLSQL - Solution Discovery · Stage 2 (WTW)No — early pipeline, too uncertain to forecast
Phase 2 — RecruitmentRecruitment (Path 2.1) · P.S (Path 2.2) · Commercial DesignYes
Phase 3 — PricingPricingYes
Phase 4 — Legal ContractingPaper Process / ContractingYes
Phase 5 — Closed WonClosed WonNo — already won, captured in cash flow directly

The phase filter strip at the top of Aureum Demand Forecast lets you toggle which phases enter the forecast on the fly. Defaults are tuned to Michael's preferences: Recruitment + Pricing + Legal Contracting ON, SQL and Closed Won OFF.

5Build & Invoice — not Close

⚡ Headcount is when people START BILLING — not when the deal closes.A 250-person engagement may close in June but ramp 35 → 70 → 70 over the following weeks. Enter those headcounts in the month they bill, not the month the contract closes. This framing applies to every tab where you type headcount by month.

6The tabs, one by one

6.1 — Overview

Your executive snapshot. KPI cards (deals, 3-month headcount, adjusted revenue, contribution $), the Anchor month picker, a 🔒 Lock {month} forecast button, the rolling 3-month window table, and the Forecast Reliability bands (M+0 / M+1 / M+2).

6.2 — Summary (Forecast Summary Statistics)

The investor-facing summary. Three account groups — Existing, New, and Total — each shown across three horizons (3-Month · Months 4-12 · Months 13-24) for four metrics: Headcount, MRR, Contribution $, Contribution %. Figures are monthly averages over each horizon, so a multi-month sum is never mistaken for a headcount. Months 13-24 is a top-down projection (the 4-12 average grown one year at the long-term rate).

Each metric appears in three layers, side by side:

  • Forecast — Horizon's own number, straight from what's built in Aureum (same deals, headcount, rates). Update Aureum and this updates too.
  • PMFF — Prior-Month Financial Forecast: Finance / the CFO types in their own financial forecast from last month, in the same units shown (headcount, dollars, or a percent like 35). It's an input, not a Horizon calculation.
  • B/(W) — Better/(Worse) = Forecast − PMFF, color-coded (green = ahead of Finance's plan, red = behind). Calculated automatically.

Existing vs New: an account is "Existing" when that client already has an Active/Expanding deal or a Closed Won; otherwise a pipeline deal is a "New" logo (first-pass rule — to confirm with Finance). Editing PMFF is manager/admin only; observers see it read-only. Per Eric, the Existing/New split and Months 13-24 are slated to turn on for 2027, and a by-month view is planned.

6.3 — Pipeline

The C-suite snapshot — the same kind of view investors see. Four KPI cards (Total Deals, Total Headcount, Total Pipeline MRR, Total Pipeline ARR) and a phase-by-phase breakdown table with deal count, HC, MRR, ARR, and % of total MRR. Click any phase row to focus the deal breakdown below on that single phase. The bottom cards segment deals by age: Healthy (<3 mo) / Active Pipeline (3-6 mo) / Slow-moving (6-12 mo) / Critical (>1 yr).

6.4 — Aureum Demand Forecast (the single input)

Where everything starts — and the only place you forecast. Every Zoho deal in the allowed stages is one row, grouped by account, across a rolling 24 months: the current year (Y1) and next year (Y2) are spread month-by-month, and a third year (Y3) is a per-deal growth % applied to Y2. The Client and Deal columns are frozen so they stay visible as you scroll. Fcst HC (editable) is the total headcount you forecast; Zoho HC and Zoho $/head are read-only references, while Fcst $/head is your editable rate that drives Fcst MRR and Fcst ARR. Type headcount by month or use the ⇶ Spread button to ramp it; the Close Date is editable to fix erroneous Zoho dates. Check Forecast to include a deal; Super-Deal is automatic at 100+ HC. Filter with the Forecast-only toggle and the phase / BDE / search controls. The navy band at the bottom totals everything by month and year — gross HC, gross MRR, and the win-/margin-adjusted rows (Adj. HC, Adj. MRR, Contribution $) — with the 26 / 27 / 28 year totals framed in gold. 3 mo. revenue and 12-Month Plan are read-only outputs of this grid.

Exporting to Excel / Google Sheets

Two buttons at the top of Aureum take the forecast out for Finance (both are read-only and available to everyone, including observers):

  • ⬇ Download Excel — a formatted .xls (bold navy header, shaded TOTAL rows). Opens in Excel; upload it to Drive to open in Google Sheets with the formatting intact.
  • 📋 Copy for Google Sheets — copies the forecast to the clipboard; paste (Ctrl+V) into a blank Google Sheet and it fills the columns.

The download is always the forecast set only — the deals with Fcst checked (Closed Won excluded), regardless of the on-screen filters — so every row counts and there are no confusing "not-in-forecast" rows. A reminder confirms the deal count before it runs, and the bottom TOTAL rows reconcile with the detail. Columns: 2026 month-by-month + totals, 2027 month-by-month + totals, and a top-down 2028 total (each deal's 2027 total × its growth %).

Numbers always match the platformThe export and the on-screen totals are computed by the same function, so they can't drift. The ✓ Verify export = table button double-checks the file against the platform to the dollar — and that same check runs automatically before every export, blocking the download/copy if anything doesn't reconcile. So a file that downloaded is a file that matched.

6.5 — 3 mo. revenue (rolling 1-3 month forecast with Lock)

Deep-dive on the next 3 months. Only deals flagged Fcst appear here. For each, across the 3-month window: HC, Proj. Rev (HC × MRR/head), Adj. Rev (× Win %), Margin $ (× Margin %).

🔒 Lock {month} forecast — once the numbers are right, click to snapshot them as an immutable commitment. The locked snapshot is what the accuracy bands compare against when actuals roll in.

Apply BDE accuracy haircut — checkbox that multiplies each cell by that BDE's historical accuracy at that horizon. Falls back to the aggregate band when a BDE has no history yet.

The Deal column is frozen here too, and the TOTALS bar stays pinned to the bottom as you scroll. The summary cards show the three numbers Finance cares about for the next 90 days: Billable HC, projected / adjusted revenue, and Margin $.

6.6 — 12-Month Plan (months 4-12 · output)

A read-only output of months 4 through 12 from the rolling forecast — edit the headcount in Aureum, not here. Revenue = HC × $/head. These months use a single standard margin % and accuracy % set by a manager in Settings (defaults 30% / 15%). The Build & Invoice reminder is reinforced here.

6.7 — Rolling Months 13-24 (long-term outlook)

No per-deal forecasting at this horizon. Take month-12 as the baseline (or the 9-month average) and a monthly growth rate (0.5%–10%); the model projects 12 months forward for the 2-year outlook Finance and the CEO use. The growth rate and baseline are manager-only controls.

6.8 — Playground (Zoho vs Manual, with approve & push)

The scenario sandbox. Side-by-side, per deal: what Zoho says today vs what you've drafted in Aureum for Headcount, MRR, Win % and Margin %. Each diff is highlighted with a Zoho → Playground arrow.

  1. Draft changes in Aureum — they show up here automatically.
  2. Approve the deals whose drafts are ready (per-row checkbox). Approval is shared with the team.
  3. Click ↗ Push approved to Zoho. The platform sends a PATCH to Zoho per approved deal — updating Headcounts, MRR, and Probability (win %). Margin stays local — it's calculated, not a Zoho field.
Push is live · Zoho is the system of recordWrite access is enabled, so ↗ Push approved to Zoho updates the real records. Each person pushing authorizes Zoho once in their own browser (Zoho pill → Disconnect → Connect → Accept). Playground and push are manager/admin only — observers don't see the push action.

6.9 — Forecast Accuracy (with embedded Trend section)

Reliability bands by horizon. Lock a forecast → wait for the month to close → Enter Actuals → bands populate as mean ± standard deviation per horizon. Defaults until history builds: M+0 = 80% ±5, M+1 = 75% ±10, M+2 = 30% ±20. A manager can pin any band (accuracy and ±) so BDEs can't move the committed numbers.

Accuracy by BDE table breaks the bands down per person at each horizon — Michael's ask: "Don is 90% accurate one month out, 80% at two months, 50% at three."

Forecast Changes — Week over Week — a table comparing this week's weekly snapshot to last week's, per deal: which deals were added, removed, or had their headcount / adjusted revenue change. Don's ask: see exactly what moved in the forecast week to week.

📥 Backfill historical data — opens a form where you enter past months by hand to seed the accuracy bands without waiting 3+ months. One row per (month, BDE), with what was forecast at start of that month / 1 month earlier / 2 months earlier, plus the actual.

At the bottom: an embedded Forecast Trend section with toggle Weekly / Monthly / Quarterly and three line charts showing Deals forecasted, Total Headcount, and Adj. Revenue over time. Solid line = forecast; dashed green line = actual (filled from Actuals + backfill). Where the lines diverge tells you where the forecast was optimistic or sandbagged.

6.10 — Super-Deals

Qualitative assessment of programs with 100+ HC. Each program is a row with editable, color-coded dropdowns: Deal Confidence, Start Readiness, Readiness Gap, Speed Sensitivity, Ramp Duration, First Phase Timing, Phase Risk Concentration, Program Horizon, Deployment Map. Edit Peak HC / Phase 1 / Activation / Notes with the ✎ icon.

6.11 — Active Customers — Actuals

Customers currently producing revenue (Active / Expanding / Aureum) plus Closed Won. Current HC, MRR/head, monthly $. HC falls back to the Zoho Headcounts field when no monthly HC is planned — marked with a small ·z beside the number.

6.12 — Settings (Stage Rules + Forecast Assumptions + Integrations)

Stage Rules — the default Win % and Target margin % by stage. A deal uses its own actual margin when set, otherwise the stage target. Margin is calculated — it is not a Zoho field.

Forecast Assumptions (months 4–12) — the standard margin %, the accuracy %, and the default $/head. Manager-only controls.

Plus: Zoho integration status + field mapping (MRR · ARR · TCV · Contract_Term_Months · Expected_Start_Date · Headcounts · Probability · Account_Status), Export / Import JSON for backup, and the ↻ Replay tour button.

★ Admin Panel (header button, admins only)

The gold "★ Admin" button next to Guide in the top-right opens the Admin Panel — visible only to managers and admins (observers don't see it). KPI cards at the top count Admins and Observers currently configured. Inside:

  • Team — the full roster: the four code-defined managers, every granted admin, and every observer — shown even before they've signed in (marked "not signed in yet"), so the access list is always complete. The Access column shows ★ ADMIN / 👁 OBSERVER / Editor.   • Errors — auto-captured Zoho REST failures with code, endpoint, message, and actor.

The levers you pull: Monthly HC drives projected revenue · $/head (override → MRR÷HC → default) is the price per head · Win % (Zoho Probability) discounts by likelihood · Margin % (target from Stage Rules, or the actual you enter) produces the cash-flow number · Anchor month slides the window · the Phase filter + Forecast checkbox decide what shows up where (Super is automatic at 100+ HC) · 🔒 Lock commits a forecast as the immutable accuracy baseline.

9Guided tour for new users

The first time anyone signs in, a guided walk-through pops up explaining each module in plain language, including a step on exporting to Excel / Google Sheets. The tour is role-aware: observers get a view-only framing and skip the editor-only tabs (Playground, Settings). Buttons: Skip tour, ← Previous, Next →, with a progress bar.

To replay the tour at any time — useful for new hires or as a refresher — go to Settings and click ↻ Replay tour. The tour switches the underlying tab as you progress so you see each module on the actual screen behind the explanation card.

11Backup

Several layers protect the data:

  • Daily auto-backup — a restore point is saved automatically on sign-in (the last 10 are kept). Restore from Settings → 🗂 Versions.
  • Snapshots — every 🔒 Lock keeps an immutable copy of that month's forecast, recoverable from Forecast Accuracy.
  • Permanent audit log — every action is recorded in an uncapped trail (★ Admin → Activity → 📜 Load full history), so changes are always traceable.
  • Manual backup — periodically export Settings → ⬇ Export all data (JSON); restore with ⬆ Import data (JSON).

Nothing mutates the shared forecast on load, and a full reset requires typing RESET — so opening the app can never wipe the team's work.
